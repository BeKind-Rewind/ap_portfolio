About Me

Budding Coder, Professional Coach, Athlete, Musician, Artist.

I grew up as a poor, nerdy, youngest-sibling of 4 without many friends in the mid-west. I had no expectations of gradure but found ways to put my curiousity and creativity to work: I learned to entertain myself finding solace in drawing, music, constructing, and video games. As early as 5-years-old (mid-80's), time with Dad often involved playing games on his work computer, the Commadore-64, experimenting with the command line code, and discovering why sticking magnets to the hard-drive was a "no-no." The interaction with the computer was an especially novel treat. No one had home computers at that time and we certainly couldn't have afforded anything like that! Every minute I was allowed to interact with it, or even watch my dad work through problems, was like a visit to Willy Wonka's Chocolate Factory.

More..

I've always had a great curiosity for learning, especially how to "make something myself," which would at the end of the day allow me to help others. When it came time to decide a college major, it was a tough choice but as an artist and musician I had decided to further my skills and understanding, graduating cum-laude in 2006 from The Ohio State with a focus in Horn and vocals. At the same time, I walked-on the OSU's varsity rowing program and had such a fundamentally enlightening experience, it sparked my passion for learning and teaching the sport to others.


I have been coaching rowing since 2006 with great successes, earning 2 NCAA national championships at the Division-1 level. For the last 5 years, I have been building up youth athletes to enjoy competitive careers and further be recruited to dream colleges of their chosing.


While it may at first glance appear these aspects of my life are very different, I have grown to know how much they overlap and support each other. There is art, music, passion, learning, constructing, de-constructing, playing, and teaching to found in each. And this is what I bring with me into the coding world. Pull back the curtain: let's figure out the "magic" and change the world.


I have always found that "we don't rise to our goals; rather, we settle to our level of work we're willing to put in;" that "hard work beats talent, when talent doesn't work hard;" and that "keeping it simple" holds true in every situation.
